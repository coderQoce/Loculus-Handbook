The Problem

The Internet Can't Support Autonomous Value Exchange

Topic 1
The Problem

Right now, internet is built for people clicking apps, not for software (agents) acting on their own.

Here's the issue

➡️ No native money layer:

The internet can send messages instantly (like chats or emails), but it can't send value (money/assets) easily without banks, payment apps, or third parties.

➡️ Too many middlemen:

Every transaction goes through platforms (PayPal, banks, marketplaces). This adds fees, delays, restrictions, and control.

➡️ Agents can't act freely:

AI agents or bots can't truly earn, pay, or trade on their own. They always need a human or centralized system to:

  • hold funds
  • approve actions
  • execute transactions

➡️ No unified identity or trust system:

There's no native way for an agent to have:

  • a wallet
  • a reputation
  • a transaction history

that works everywhere on internet

The internet lets information move freely, but not value and definitely not autonomous value.
